The Argument

As one reads “The Yellow Wallpaper” you might notice that the women in the story is also the narrator. One might also notice that the women/narrator is never named. The narrator of this story is also the writer, Charlotte Perkins Gilman. And here’s why Charlotte is the the women in “The Yellow Wallpaper”.

The women in the story has been diagnosed by her husband and doctor whos named John. She’s diagnosed with “Temporary nervous depression” and her treatment is bedrest. Charlotte who was also diagnosed with “nervous prostration” or nervous depression received the same treatment, bedrest [2].The reason for the depression is unclear for both the Narrator and Charlotte.

The Charlotte and the Narrator were not able to take care of their homesteads, this is due to the treatment only allowing limited physical activity. Cynthia writes in the book ‘Charlotte Perkins Gilman: A Biography” “The couple hired a woman to help with the housework during the first three months of March” [3] In “The Yellow Wallpaper”, “There comes John’s sister. She is a perfect and enthusiastic housekeeper and hopes for no better profession” [1] Both the Narrator and Charlotte had housekeepers during the descent of their mental health. Charlotte and the Narrator lives are starting to line up with one another, even what they wanted to do with their time is similar.

Libal notes in the book “History of Therapy and Drug Treatment”, that in the issue of the Forerunner which Charlotte explains why she wrote “The Yellow Wallpaper”.“Charlotte was given the bedrest cure to treat her nervous disease. She was only allowed to have two hours of intellectual life a day and was never to touch a pen, brush or pencil again for as long as she lived”[2] In “The Yellow Wallpaper”, “There comes John, and I must put this away, - he hates to have me write a word”[1]. The Narrator is Charlotte and this is her story.

Charlotte wanted to expose just how bad the bedrest cure was for her. She wanted to show how it made here decrease even more into depression. She wanted to help other women who were given this treatment or thinking about doing the treatment. She wanted them to have an inside look to what destruction it can do to someone, as a warning.

Key Takeaways

  • Charlotte Perkins Gilman is the Narrator.
  • Charlotte created the story to help other women, and to inform them of the dangers of bedrest treatment.
